Overview

The Psychology of Political Behavior Studies (PPBS) are an ongoing series of quota-based national cross-sections containing a set of repeating core questions on social and political attitudes, values, voting and candidate preferences, and political participation.

PPBS leverages 12 datasets, collected through professional survey companies, for a combined N of 21,107 interviews. It includes four nationally representative cross-sections of the American population (N=7,259) during the last three elections in the US, their replication/confirmatory analogues (N=11,582), and repeated measures recontacts (N=2,215) across multiple time-points, allowing for both within and between research designs.

Section IProject Overview

The Psychology of Political Behavior Studies (PPBS) are an ongoing series of quota-based national cross-sections containing a set of repeating core questions on social and political attitudes, values, voting and candidate preferences, and political participation.

PPBS leverages 12 datasets, collected through professional survey companies, for a combined N of 21,107 interviews. It includes four nationally representative cross-sections of the American population (N=7,259) during the last three elections in the US (Presidential 2016 and 2020, and Midterms 2018), their replication/confirmatory analogues (N=11,582) in pre- and post-election studies, with added survey-specific repeated measures recontacts (N=2,215) across three time-points, allowing for both within and between research designs.

Taken together, PPBS has surveyed more than 60 political and psychological constructs, many using full instruments, allowing for the testing of competing explanations of the psychology of political behavior.
